Bestiary

Cynx

Known by its more common name, the Autumn Jaguar, the Cynx ambushes prey that make their way through the surrounding forests that exist around the outskirts of Autumn. Like many creatures native to the Dawn lands, the Cynx's coat is covered in malignant sun spots that provide an even greater degree of natural camouflage. An experienced adventurer can tell the age and strength of a cynx by the amount of discoloration in their fur.

The Cynx utilizes its stealth and powerful claws to attack unsuspecting prey. While dangerous to a lone adventurer, most Cynx are skittish when faced with multiple foes, so they are rarely a danger to a group. When cornered, or facing starvation, they have been known to attack groups often targeting the most imposing figure first in an effort to gain the upper hand. True to its name, Cynx's mainly reside around the outskirts of major cities and are a rare sight in the deeper reaches of the Dawn lands and prefer to hunt where smaller, natural creatures reside such as rabbits or deer.

Natural Abilities

  1. Enhanced movement speed (90 feet).
  2. Can initiate a new stealth check if it forgoes its attack action at the end of a round.
  3. Can take an extra attack action, bite, if it successfully pounces an opponent.

Goblin

Goblins reside in the mountains throughout the inhabitable reaches of the world. Though they are capable of forming small bands and tribes, Goblins value power more than the other sentient races and often betray one another in order to further their own ambitions. Because of this, Goblins face difficulties forming a true society and usually only band together to fend off a common threat to their mountain strongholds. Goblins especially hate Dwarves and reject the rights that the Dwarves claim to the mining rights of the mountain territories.

Because of the terrain, Goblins primarily make use of spears and crossbows in combat. Larger tribes also possess siege weaponry such as ballistas for both assaulting other Goblin fortresses. Despite their treachery, Goblins can work together and use basic tactics. If they perceive a numbers advantage, they attack brazenly; however, they are equally capable of guerrilla tactics to take down more powerful foes. The opportunistic qualities of the Goblins make them a notable threat in the mountainous territories of the realm.

Natural Abilities

  1. Gains a +1 flank bonus to hit if another Goblin targets the same enemy in the same turn.
  2. Proficiency in spears, crossbows, and Goblin siege weaponry.
  3. Does not incur a movement penalty in difficult terrain caused in mountain territories.

Jaeger

Also known as the Ghost Wolf, Jaegers are found deep within the Dusk lands. Little is known of their origin and capabilities. It is said that if one is bitten by a Jaeger, then they will become one within a period of three days if left untreated by a purification spell of a Bishop. Because very few people have seen a true Jaeger, reports of their appearance vary greatly which has called their existence into question. Regardless of inconsistencies, all reports agree they are a creature to be avoided at all costs.

Because they appear as wolves, most people believe they hunt in packs. Aside from their poisonous bite, Jaegers can attack with their claws and are said to possess a hallucinogenic gaze. The gaze distorts reality giving them their appearance an ominous shadow that can distort the aim of enemies. Legends of the Jaegers allude to the existence of a den mother referred to as the Okami. It is said to rule the Dusk lands and that any adventurer capable of defeating her would gain her power and become the new ruler of her realm.

Natural Abilities

  1. Hypnotic gaze causes disadvantage to-hit for one chosen enemy per turn as a free action.
  2. Venomous bite. A failed save causes the enemy to contract the disease known as Lupin's decay.
  3. Can multi-attack with both its bite and claw attacks in a single turn.
